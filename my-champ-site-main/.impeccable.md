## Design Context

### Users
Motion designers, creative directors, ad agencies, and direct clients looking to commission animated illustrations, looping GIFs, and motion design work. They visit the portfolio to evaluate Aleven's style, see past work, and potentially reach out for collaboration. Visiting context: desktop-first (creative professionals browse portfolios on workstations), but mobile must be polished for quick shares and social traffic.

### Brand Personality
**Elegant · Dark · Creative** — The work speaks through motion, and the site should feel like a gallery: the interface recedes, the work commands attention. Confident, not loud.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: [pleasecallmechamp.com](https://pleasecallmechamp.com/) — oversized monochrome typography as architectural element, deep black space, asymmetric project grids, vibrant project thumbnails as the only color, generous negative space, flush-edge alignments.
- **Theme**: Both dark and light with a toggle. Dark is the default — it's the natural viewing context for creative professionals and matches the brand personality.
- **Anti-references**: Generic portfolio templates with card grids, gradient text, glassmorphism, neon-on-dark "tech" aesthetic.

### Design Principles
1. **Space is the primary design material** — Generous black/white negative space between sections creates gallery-like pacing. Let the work breathe.
2. **Typography as architecture** — Oversized type creates structural landmarks. The hierarchy should be dramatic: massive headings, restrained body text.
3. **The work is the color** — The interface stays monochrome; project thumbnails and animations provide all the vibrancy.
4. **Asymmetry over symmetry** — Break predictable centered layouts with intentional left-alignment and varied grid spans.
5. **Restraint over decoration** — No unnecessary borders, shadows, or containers. Every visual element must earn its place.
